Toasted Golden Milk Spice Mix

This is my go-to for shutting down late-night sugar cravings and soothing an inflamed gut. Turmeric is the star here, but its active compound, curcumin, is notoriously hard for your body to absorb on its own. Toasting the spices gently wakes up their volatile oils, and the black pepper is non-negotiable—it increases curcumin absorption by up to 2,000 percent.

Prep time: 5 mins | Cook time: 3 mins | Servings: 24

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup ground turmeric
  • 2 tbsp ground ginger
  • 2 tsp ground Ceylon cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp ground cardamom
  • 1/4 tsp ground nutmeg

Instructions

1. Place a dry skillet over low heat and add the turmeric, ginger, cinnamon, black pepper, cardamom, and nutmeg. 2. Toast the spices for 2 to 3 minutes, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon. Do not walk away. The moment they release a rich, warm aroma, they are done. 3. Remove the skillet from the heat immediately and transfer the spices to a ceramic plate to cool completely so they don't keep cooking on the hot pan. 4. Once cool, transfer the mixture to an airtight glass jar and store in a cool, dark pantry.

Chef's Tip

Always use Ceylon cinnamon instead of Cassia cinnamon; Ceylon contains far lower levels of coumarin, a compound that can be toxic to the liver in high doses.

Hormone-Balancing Seed Cycling Mix

Seed cycling is a gentle, food-based practice designed to support natural estrogen and progesterone patterns. This dual-phase mix utilizes organic, raw seeds to provide the specific lignans and essential fatty acids needed during different phases of your cycle. It adds a pleasant, nutty texture to morning coconut yogurt or chia puddings.

Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 0 mins | Servings: 14

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup raw organic pumpkin seeds
  • 1/2 cup raw organic flaxseeds
  • 1/2 cup raw organic sesame seeds
  • 1/2 cup raw organic sunflower seeds

Instructions

1. Divide your seeds into two separate batches based on your cycle phase: combine the pumpkin and flaxseeds for Phase 1 (days 1-14, follicular), and the sesame and sunflower seeds for Phase 2 (days 15-28, luteal). 2. Place the seeds for your current phase into a high-speed blender or clean spice grinder. 3. This is non-negotiable—it protects the delicate, easily oxidized omega-3 fatty acids from going rancid.

Chef's Tip

Grinding the seeds is non-negotiable; the human digestive tract cannot break down the tough outer hulls of flax and sesame seeds, meaning you miss out on their hormone-balancing lignans if eaten whole.

Fermented Garlic & Ginger Paste

This is gut health in a jar. By lacto-fermenting garlic and ginger, you're essentially pre-digesting them. The raw honey and salt feed the beneficial bacteria, turning this aromatic paste into a probiotic powerhouse that gives an instant, savory depth to anything you cook.

Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 0 mins | Servings: 32

Ingredients

  • 1 cup peeled garlic cloves
  • 1 cup fresh ginger root, washed and sliced
  • 1 tbsp coarse sea salt
  • 2 tbsp raw unpasteurized honey
  • 2 tbsp filtered water

Instructions

1. Place the garlic cloves, sliced ginger, sea salt, raw honey, and filtered water into a food processor. 2. Process the ingredients on high speed, scraping down the sides as needed, until a smooth, uniform paste forms. 3. Transfer the paste into a sterilized glass mason jar, leaving at least an inch of headspace at the top to allow for fermentation gases. 4. Close the lid loosely and let the jar sit on your kitchen counter at room temperature for 3 to 5 days, burping the lid daily to release pressure, before tightening the lid and storing in the refrigerator.

Chef's Tip

If a thin layer of liquid separates at the top of the jar during fermentation, do not discard it; simply stir it back into the paste with a clean, dry spoon before using.

Homemade Ghee

Ghee is pure liquid gold. By simmering grass-fed butter and removing the water and milk solids, you're left with a clean, high-smoke-point fat that is rich in butyric acid—the primary fuel source for your gut cells. It's incredibly soothing to a damaged gut lining.

Prep time: 5 mins | Cook time: 25 mins | Servings: 32

Ingredients

  • 1 lb unsalted grass-fed butter

Instructions

1. Cut the butter into uniform cubes and place them in a heavy-bottomed saucepan over medium-low heat. 2. Allow the butter to melt completely without stirring, letting it come to a gentle simmer as foam begins to rise to the surface. 3. Continue simmering for 15 to 20 minutes; the milk solids will sink to the bottom of the pan and begin to turn a rich, golden brown, while the liquid becomes completely clear. 4. Strain the hot liquid carefully through a fine-mesh sieve lined with double-layered cheesecloth into a clean, dry glass jar, leaving the browned milk solids behind.

Chef's Tip

Do not walk away from the stove during the final five minutes of cooking; the transition from perfectly caramelized, nutty ghee to burnt, bitter fat happens in a matter of seconds.

DIY Healing Bone Broth (Nước Dùng Xương)

This is my take on a traditional Vietnamese broth, prioritizing a clean, crystal-clear finish over the murky, muddy broths you often see online. We blanch the bones first to get rid of the impurities, then char the aromatics for a deep, smoky undertone. It’s pure medicine for your gut barrier, packed with collagen, glycine, and gut-healing amino acids.

Prep time: 20 mins | Cook time: 12 hours | Servings: 8

Ingredients

  • 3 lbs grass-fed beef marrow and knuckle bones
  • 1 large yellow onion, unpeeled and halved
  • 3 inches fresh ginger root, halved lengthwise
  • 2 star anise pods
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 tbsp premium fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp sea salt
  • 12 cups filtered water

Instructions

1. Place the raw beef bones in a large stockpot, cover with cold tap water, and bring to a rolling boil over high heat for 10 minutes to draw out all grey scum and impurities. 2. Drain the bones completely in a colander, discard the dirty water, and rinse each bone thoroughly under cold running water to remove any remaining residue. 3. While rinsing, char the onion halves and ginger over an open flame or under a broiler for 5 to 7 minutes until the skins are blackened and fragrant. 4. Return the blanched bones to the clean stockpot, add the charred onion, charred ginger, star anise, cinnamon stick, fish sauce, sea salt, and 12 cups of fresh filtered water. 5. Bring the pot to a gentle simmer over medium-low heat, then reduce the heat to the absolute lowest setting, cover with a lid cracked slightly, and let it simmer gently for 12 hours (skimming any foam from the surface occasionally). 6. Strain the rich broth through a fine-mesh strainer lined with cheesecloth into heat-safe glass jars, discard the solids, and let the broth cool completely before storing in the refrigerator.

Chef's Tip

Never let the broth come to a rapid boil after the initial blanching phase; a rolling boil emulsifies the fat and marrow into the water, resulting in a cloudy, greasy broth instead of a clean, mineral-rich, clear tonic.

Avocado & Spinach Egg Muffins

These are your emergency response team for chaotic Mondays. Bake a double batch on Sunday night. They’re cold, they’re dense, they’re packed with monounsaturated fats that keep your blood sugar dead-flat for hours. No sugar crashes, no mid-morning brain fog. Just steady, reliable energy.

Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 20 mins | Servings: 3

Ingredients

  • 6 large free-range eggs
  • 2 tablespoons unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 cup fresh baby spinach, chopped
  • 1 ripe avocado, pitted and diced
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, quartered
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper

Instructions

1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a six-cup muffin tin with a small amount of avocado oil or use silicone baking liners. 2. In a medium mixing bowl, whisk the eggs, unsweetened almond milk, garlic powder, sea salt, and black pepper together until fully combined and slightly frothy. 3. Divide the chopped spinach, diced avocado, and quartered cherry tomatoes evenly among the six muffin cups. 4. Pour the whisked egg mixture over the vegetables in each cup, filling them about three-quarters of the way to the top. 5. Bake for 18 to 20 minutes, or until the muffins are set in the center and lightly golden on top. 6. Allow them to cool in the pan for 5 minutes before removing and serving warm.

Key Takeaway

Use silicone baking cups instead of paper liners to prevent the eggs from sticking and to ensure a perfect, mess-free release every time.

Warming Turmeric & Kale Scramble

Turmeric is a miracle worker for systemic inflammation, but here is the thing most people miss: curcumin (its active compound) is virtually useless on its own. Your body can't absorb it. You need fat and a pinch of black pepper to unlock its benefits. This quick, earthy scramble does exactly that.

Prep time: 5 mins | Cook time: 5 mins | Servings: 2

Ingredients

  • 4 large free-range eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 pinch freshly cracked black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon virgin coconut oil
  • 1 cup baby kale, tough stems removed
  • 2 tablespoons nutritional yeast
  • 1/4 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1 tablespoon fresh chives, minced

Instructions

1. In a small bowl, crack the eggs and add the ground turmeric, black pepper, and sea salt. Whisk vigorously until the turmeric is completely integrated and no clumps remain. 2. Melt the coconut oil in a non-stick skillet over medium-low heat. 3. Add the baby kale to the skillet and sauté for 1 to 2 minutes until it is just wilted and bright green. 4. Reduce the heat to low and pour the whisked egg mixture into the skillet over the wilted kale. 5. Using a spatula, gently fold the eggs from the edges of the pan toward the center, allowing soft curds to form. 6. Just before the eggs are fully set, sprinkle the nutritional yeast over the scramble and fold once more. 7. Remove from the heat, garnish with minced fresh chives, and serve immediately.

Key Takeaway

Never skip the black pepper in this dish; the piperine in black pepper increases the bioavailability of turmeric's anti-inflammatory curcumin by up to 2,000 percent.

Smoked Salmon & Asparagus Frittata

High-quality smoked salmon provides crucial omega-3 fatty acids that actively lower cortisol levels and combat cellular inflammation. Paired with prebiotic-rich asparagus, this elegant frittata supports gut health, which is directly linked to your body's stress response. It is a nutrient-dense, savory masterpiece suitable for a quiet weekend brunch or weekday meal prep.

Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 25 mins | Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 8 large free-range eggs
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened coconut milk (carton)
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 cup asparagus spears, woody ends snapped off and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 3 ounces wild-caught smoked salmon, torn into bite-sized ribbons
  • 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper

Instructions

1. Preheat your oven's broiler to high. 2. In a large bowl, whisk the eggs, unsweetened coconut milk, chopped dill, sea salt, and black pepper together until smooth. 3. Heat the olive oil in a 10-inch ovenproof skillet (such as cast iron) over medium heat. 4. Add the chopped red onion and asparagus pieces to the skillet. Sauté for 4 to 5 minutes, or until the asparagus is tender-crisp and the onion is translucent. 5. Distribute the smoked salmon ribbons evenly across the skillet among the sautéed vegetables. 6. Pour the whisked egg mixture into the skillet, ensuring it covers the salmon and vegetables evenly. Let the mixture cook undisturbed on the stovetop for 5 to 6 minutes, until the bottom and sides are set but the top is still runny. 7. Transfer the entire skillet to the preheated oven under the broiler. Cook for 3 to 4 minutes, keeping a close eye on it, until the top is puffed, golden brown, and completely set in the center. 8. Slice into wedges and serve warm.

Key Takeaway

To keep the eggs exceptionally tender and custardy, remove the frittata from the broiler while the center still has a tiny, almost imperceptible jiggle; it will finish cooking on the counter from residual heat.

Chilled Coconut-Chia Berry Parfait

If the thought of eating a hot, heavy breakfast makes your stomach turn first thing in the morning, this is your solution. Chia seeds swell up in your gut, acting like a slow-release hydration pack that gently sweeps excess hormones out of your system without sending insulin soaring.

Top each parfait with toasted shredded coconut and raw pumpkin seeds for a satisfying, mineral-dense crunch.

Earthy Maca & Pumpkin Seed Oatmeal

Top each bowl with raw pumpkin seeds and a drizzle of raw pumpkin seed butter if desired.

Warm Quinoa & Poached Egg Bowl

Stepping away from sweet breakfasts is the single fastest way to heal your adrenals. If you have never had a savory grain bowl for breakfast, prepare to have your mind blown. The warm, runny yolk of a poached egg mixes with cooked quinoa to create a rich, comforting sauce.

Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 15 mins | Servings: 2

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cooked quinoa, warmed
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 cups baby Swiss chard, chopped
  • 1/2 cup shiitake mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 large free-range eggs
  • 2 tablespoons green onions, finely sliced
  • 1/4 teaspoon sea salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar (for poaching)

Instructions

1. Heat the olive oil in a medium skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced shiitake mushrooms and sauté for 4 to 5 minutes until they are tender and slightly browned. 2. Add the chopped Swiss chard to the skillet and sauté for another 2 minutes until just wilted. Season the greens and mushrooms with a pinch of sea salt and black pepper, then remove from heat. 3. To poach the eggs, bring a medium saucepan filled with 3 inches of water and 1 tablespoon of apple cider vinegar to a gentle simmer. 4. Crack one egg into a small ramekin. Gently swirl the simmering water with a spoon to create a slow whirlpool, then slip the egg into the center of the whirlpool. 5. Cook for 3 minutes until the egg white is fully set but the yolk remains soft. Remove with a slotted spoon and repeat with the second egg. 6. Divide the warm cooked quinoa between two breakfast bowls. 7. Arrange the sautéed mushrooms and chard on top of the quinoa. 8. Place one poached egg in the center of each bowl, garnish with sliced green onions, and season with remaining black pepper.

Key Takeaway

Cook your quinoa in organic chicken or beef bone broth instead of plain water to infuse the grain with gut-healing collagen and a rich, savory flavor profile.

  • 5 drops liquid stevia or monk fruit extract (to taste)

Instructions

1. Combine the frozen wild berries, fresh lemon juice, lemon zest, and water in a small, non-reactive saucepan over medium heat. 2. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cook for 8 to 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the berries break down completely and the liquid reduces into a light syrup. 3. Remove the saucepan from the heat source and allow the mixture to cool for 5 minutes. 4. Whisk the ashwagandha powder and liquid stevia directly into the warm berry mixture until completely dissolved and free of lumps. 5. For a perfectly smooth glaze, press the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve using the back of a spoon to remove the seeds. Alternatively, leave it rustic and chunky. Let cool completely before serving.

Chef's Tip

Adaptogenic herbs are delicate. Exposure to rolling, boiling heat will ruin the therapeutic compounds that help your body manage stress. Always wait to whisk your ashwagandha or reishi powder into the pot until after you have pulled it off the stove and let it cool down slightly.

Spicy Ginger-Turmeric Golden Elixir

Bright golden and aromatic, this warm dressing is built around the highly bioactive synergy of curcumin and gingerol. Chronic, low-grade inflammation acts as a constant physical stressor on the adrenal cortex; this emulsion helps interrupt that inflammatory loop at the cellular level. It provides an earthy, spicy warmth to steamed greens or grain bowls.

Prep time: 5 mins | Cook time: 2 mins | Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger root, peeled and finely grated
  • 1 tbsp fresh turmeric root, peeled and finely grated (or 1 tsp ground turmeric)
  • 1/4 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/3 cup cold-pressed avocado oil
  • 2 tbsp raw apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp raw honey (or 5 drops liquid monk fruit for a strict sugar-free option)
  • 1/2 tsp fine sea salt

Instructions

1. Place the grated ginger, grated turmeric, black pepper, apple cider vinegar, raw honey, and sea salt in a small glass bowl. 2. Whisk the ingredients together until the salt and honey are completely dissolved. 3. Slowly drizzle in the avocado oil in a thin, continuous stream while whisking rapidly to create a temporary emulsion. 4. If you prefer a completely smooth drizzle, transfer the mixture to a small blender and blend on high for 30 seconds. 5. Serve immediately over warm dishes, or store in a sealed glass container in the refrigerator for up to five days. Warm to room temperature and shake vigorously before using.

Chef's Tip

Curcumin is a metabolic powerhouse, but your body is notoriously bad at absorbing it on its own. Never skip the black pepper in this recipe. The piperine inside black pepper increases your body's absorption of turmeric's active compounds by up to 2,000 percent.

Savoury Garlic-Herb Aioli

This rich, emulsified condiment brings Mediterranean flare and a healthy dose of raw garlic to your kitchen. Garlic provides diallyl disulfide, an organosulfur compound that supports liver phase II detoxification pathways—crucial for clearing excess circulating hormones and cortisol metabolites from your bloodstream.

Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 0 mins | Servings: 8

Ingredients

  • 1 large egg yolk, room temperature (pasture-raised)
  • 3/4 cup avocado oil (do not use olive oil, as it turns bitter when blended)
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely grated on a microplane
  • 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp fresh chives, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
  • 1/2 tsp fine sea salt

Instructions

1. Ensure all your ingredients, especially the egg yolk, are at room temperature to guarantee a stable emulsion. 2. Place the egg yolk, grated garlic, lemon juice, and sea salt in the bottom of a tall immersion blender cup or a medium glass bowl. 3. If using an immersion blender, pour all of the avocado oil directly on top of the yolk mixture. Lower the blender blade all the way to the bottom of the cup, turn it on high, and hold it completely still for 5 seconds as a thick white emulsion forms. Slowly pull the blender upward to incorporate the remaining oil. 4. If whisking by hand, slowly drip the avocado oil into the yolk mixture, whisking constantly and rapidly. Do not rush; adding the oil too quickly will break the emulsion. 5. Once a thick, glossy aioli forms, gently fold in the chopped chives and parsley with a spatula. Transfer to a clean jar and refrigerate immediately; consume within three days.

Chef's Tip

If your aioli splits into an oily, curdled mess, do not throw it out. Simply whisk a single teaspoon of warm water in a clean bowl, then slowly, drop by drop, whisk the broken aioli into it. It will pull right back together.

Umami-Rich Coconut Stir-Fry Glaze

This savory glaze utilizes coconut aminos to deliver a rich soy-free umami flavor. Standard soy sauce contains processed phytoestrogens and gluten, which can trigger immune sensitivities and disrupt hormonal balance. Coconut aminos bypass these issues while providing a natural source of potassium and trace minerals to replenish weary adrenals.

Prep time: 5 mins | Cook time: 5 mins | Servings: 6

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup organic coconut aminos
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger root, finely grated
  • 1 tsp arrowroot powder (for thickening)
  • 1/4 cup organic beef or chicken bone broth
  • 1/4 tsp crushed red pepper flakes (optional)

Instructions

1. In a small bowl, whisk the arrowroot powder into the cold bone broth until completely dissolved, creating a smooth slurry. Set aside. 2. In a small saucepan over medium heat, combine the coconut aminos, toasted sesame oil, minced garlic, grated ginger, and red pepper flakes. 3. Bring the mixture to a bare simmer for 2 minutes, allowing the aromatic ginger and garlic to soften and release their essential oils into the glaze. 4. Stir the arrowroot slurry once more to ensure it is fully suspended, then pour it slowly into the simmering saucepan while whisking continuously. 5. Keep whisking for 1 to 2 minutes as the glaze comes back to a gentle simmer. The liquid will transform from cloudy to clear and will thicken into a rich, glossy glaze that coats the back of a spoon. Remove from heat and let cool slightly before using.

Chef's Tip

Because coconut aminos are made from fermented coconut sap, they have a natural, deep sweetness that mimics teriyaki sauce. This allows you to get that sticky, caramelized glaze on your stir-fry without dumping in cups of refined white sugar.
