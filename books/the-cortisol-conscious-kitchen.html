Haldi Ka Doodh Paste (Ashwagandha Golden Milk Paste)

This warm, earthy paste comes straight from ancient Ayurvedic traditions. It pairs the heavy-hitting, anti-inflammatory power of turmeric with ashwagandha—a plant helper that keeps your body from going into overdrive when you're stressed to the bone. Here is a quick science tip: you cannot skip the black pepper. The piperine in black pepper increases how much turmeric your body can actually absorb by up to 2,000 percent. Keep a jar of this in the back of your fridge. When your day goes sideways, whisk a spoonful into warm milk for an instant physical anchor.

Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 15 mins | Servings: 24 (1 tsp per serving)

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup organic turmeric powder
  • 2 tbsp organic ashwagandha root powder
  • 1 tbsp ground ginger
  • 1 tsp freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 tsp ground cardamom
  • 1/2 cup filtered water (plus extra if needed)
  • 1/3 cup cold-pressed virgin coconut oil or ghee

Instructions

1. Whisk the turmeric, ashwagandha, ginger, black pepper, and cardamom together in a small saucepan. 2. Pour in the water and place the saucepan over low heat. Cook, stirring constantly with a silicone spatula, for 8 to 10 minutes until a thick, smooth paste forms. If the paste becomes too dry or chalky, add an extra tablespoon of water. 3. Remove the pan from the heat and let it cool for 5 minutes. 4. Stir in the coconut oil or ghee until completely melted and integrated into the paste. 5. Transfer the mixture to a clean, sterilized glass jar and seal tightly. Store in the refrigerator for up to 3 weeks.

Chef's Tip

To make a quick warming tonic, whisk 1 teaspoon of this paste into 1 cup of warm, unsweetened almond or coconut milk, and sweeten with a touch of raw honey.

Adaptogenic Shiitake & Reishi Bouillon

Throw out those salty, chemical-heavy bouillon cubes from the supermarket. Seriously, toss them. This homemade, savory powder uses dried shiitake and reishi mushrooms to soothe tired adrenal glands and help you get actual, restorative sleep. It dissolves almost instantly in boiling water. You can use it as a rich base for your weeknight soups, or just drink it straight from a mug like a savory, grounding tea when you need a minute to breathe.

Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 0 mins | Servings: 32 (1 tbsp per serving)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dehydrated shiitake mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup dehydrated porcini mushrooms
  • 2 tbsp organic reishi mushroom powder
  • 1/2 cup nutritional yeast
  • 2 tbsp Celtic sea salt
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp dried thyme leaves
  • 1/2 tsp rubbed sage

Instructions

1. Ensure your high-speed blender or spice grinder is completely dry before beginning. 2. Place the dehydrated shiitake and porcini mushrooms into the blender canister and pulse until they are reduced to a fine powder. 3. Add the reishi powder, nutritional yeast, Celtic sea salt, garlic powder, onion powder, dried thyme, and rubbed sage. 4. Blend on high speed for 30 to 45 seconds until all ingredients are pulverized into a uniform, fine dust. 5. Let the dust settle in the blender container for a minute before opening the lid to avoid inhaling the fine powder. 6. Transfer the bouillon powder to an airtight glass jar. Store in a cool, dark pantry for up to 3 months.

Chef's Tip

To use, dissolve 1 tablespoon of the bouillon powder into 8 ounces of boiling water for an instant, adrenal-supportive sipping broth.

Black Sesame & Kelp Gomasio

This simple Japanese condiment swaps out the usual white sesame seeds for black ones. In traditional eastern medicine, black sesame seeds are highly valued for boosting energy and fighting deep, bone-weary fatigue. When you mix them with iodine-dense kelp and unrefined pink salt, you get a savory sprinkle that delivers the basic trace minerals your adrenals desperately need to keep your hormones in check.

Prep time: 5 mins | Cook time: 8 mins | Servings: 16 (1 tbsp per serving)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup raw black sesame seeds
  • 1 tbsp kelp granules or dulse flakes
  • 1 tbsp coarse Himalayan pink salt

Instructions

1. Rinse the black sesame seeds in a fine-mesh sieve and drain them thoroughly. 2. Heat a dry, heavy-bottomed cast-iron skillet over medium-low heat. Add the wet sesame seeds to the pan. 3. Toast the seeds, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon, for about 5 to 7 minutes. They will begin to pop and release a deep, nutty aroma. The moisture must be fully evaporated. 4. Remove the skillet from the heat and stir in the kelp granules and pink salt, letting the residual heat of the pan toast the salt and kelp slightly. 5. Transfer the mixture to a mortar and pestle or a clean food processor. 6. Grind or pulse gently until about half of the seeds are crushed and fragrant. Do not overprocess, or you will end up with sesame paste. 7. Allow the mixture to cool completely before storing in an airtight jar. Keep in the refrigerator to protect the delicate oils.

Chef's Tip

Sprinkle this liberally over steamed vegetables, avocado toast, or soft-boiled eggs for an instant mineral and flavor boost.

Canh Xương (Reishi-Infused Bone Broth)

Think of this slow-simmered, collagen-packed bone broth as a reset button for your gut and your nerves. Simmering the bones low and slow extracts amino acids like glycine, which acts like a physical brake pedal for a racing mind. By adding reishi mushrooms and warm spices like star anise, you get a savory, comforting broth that actively coaxes your cortisol levels back down to earth. It is a warm hug for your nervous system.

Prep time: 20 mins | Cook time: 12 hours | Servings: 8

Ingredients

  • 3 lbs grass-fed beef marrow and knuckle bones
  • 1 medium onion, halved and charred
  • 3 inches fresh ginger, sliced and bruised
  • 2 whole star anise
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2 dried reishi mushroom slices
  • 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp Celtic sea salt
  • 12 cups filtered water

Instructions

1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Arrange the beef bones on a roasting sheet and roast for 30 minutes until deeply browned. 2. While the bones roast, place the halved onion and bruised ginger slices directly over an open flame on your stove, or under your oven's broiler, until slightly charred around the edges. 3. Transfer the roasted bones, charred onion, and ginger into a large stockpot or a slow cooker. 4. Add the star anise, cinnamon stick, reishi mushroom slices, apple cider vinegar, and Celtic sea salt. 5. Pour in the filtered water, ensuring all the bones are fully submerged. Let the mixture sit at room temperature for 20 minutes to allow the vinegar to help extract minerals from the bones. 6. Bring the pot to a boil over high heat, then immediately reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer gently for at least 12 hours (or up to 24 hours). If using a slow cooker, set it to low for 12 hours. 7. Skim off any foam or impurities that rise to the surface during the first hour of cooking. 8. Remove from the heat, strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve lined with cheesecloth into large glass jars, and let cool. Once cold, discard the hardened fat cap from the top of the jars before using.

Chef's Tip

The apple cider vinegar is key because its acidity breaks down the bone matrix, pulling out the maximum amount of magnesium, calcium, and collagen into the liquid.

Cacao Maca Dust (Cortisol-Conscious Hot Cocoa Base)

Most store-bought hot cocoa mixes are just sugar bombs that spike your insulin and send your stress hormones into overdrive. This rich, dark powder does the opposite. We use raw cacao—which is naturally loaded with anxiety-busting magnesium—and blend it with gelatinized maca root to support your hormone system. It satisfies that deep chocolate craving while gently smoothing out your frayed edges.

Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 0 mins | Servings: 12 (2 tbsp per serving)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup organic raw cacao powder
  • 4 tbsp gelatinized maca powder
  • 2 tbsp ground Ceylon cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp ground cardamom
  • 1/2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1/4 cup monk fruit sweetener (optional)

Instructions

1. Sift the raw cacao powder through a fine-mesh strainer into a medium mixing bowl to eliminate any lumps. 2. Add the gelatinized maca powder, Ceylon cinnamon, ground nutmeg, ground cardamom, and sea salt. 3. If using monk fruit sweetener, add it to the bowl. Whisk all the ingredients together vigorously for 2 minutes to ensure an even distribution of the spices and adaptogens. 4. Transfer the mixture to a clean, dry, airtight glass jar. Store in a cool, dry cupboard for up to 6 months.

Chef's Tip

Always use gelatinized maca instead of raw maca. The gelatinization process removes the hard-to-digest starches, making the adaptogens highly bioavailable and gentle on your digestive tract.

Rosemary-Infused Cold-Pressed Olive Oil

Internal inflammation is a sneaky trigger that keeps your cortisol high. This simple kitchen oil uses cold-pressed extra virgin olive oil to carry the powerful antioxidants found in fresh rosemary directly to your cells. By using a gentle, low-temperature heating method, we protect the fragile, healthy fats in the olive oil while drawing out that beautiful, woodsy rosemary aroma.

Prep time: 5 mins | Cook time: 30 mins | Servings: 32 (1 tbsp per serving)

Ingredients

  • 4 fresh rosemary sprigs (washed and dried completely)
  • 2 cups cold-pressed extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp whole black peppercorns

Instructions

1. Ensure the rosemary sprigs are 100% dry; any moisture can cause mold or bacterial growth in the oil. Bruise the rosemary sprigs slightly with the back of a knife to release their essential oils. 2. Place the bruised rosemary and black peppercorns in a small, heavy saucepan. 3. Pour the extra virgin olive oil over the rosemary, ensuring the sprigs are completely submerged. 4. Place the saucepan over the lowest heat setting on your stove. Let the oil warm gently for about 30 minutes. The oil should never bubble or simmer; keep the temperature low to preserve its structural integrity. 5. Remove the pan from the heat and let the oil cool to room temperature with the rosemary still infusing. 6. Once cool, strain the oil through a fine-mesh sieve into a dark glass bottle. You can place a fresh, completely dry sprig of rosemary inside the bottle for visual appeal if desired. Store in a cool, dark cabinet and use within 1 month.

Chef's Tip

Always store infused olive oil in a dark glass bottle in a cool place, as light and heat rapidly degrade the delicate fats and antioxidant compounds.

Activated Pepita Butter (Sprouted Pumpkin Seed Butter)

Pumpkin seeds are a goldmine for magnesium and zinc—two minerals that your body burns through at warp speed when you're stressed. This rich, green seed butter uses sprouted seeds to bypass phytic acid (a natural compound that blocks mineral absorption). Because the seeds are sprouted, your gut can easily absorb the nutrients and use them to rebuild your depleted mineral reserves.

Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 10 mins | Servings: 16 (2 tbsp per serving)

Ingredients

  • 3 cups sprouted (activated) pumpkin seeds (pepitas)
  • 2 tbsp avocado oil or liquid coconut oil
  • 1 tsp Celtic sea salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground vanilla bean (optional)

Instructions

1. Preheat your oven to 320°F (160°C). 2. Spread the sprouted pumpkin seeds in a single layer on a rimmed baking sheet. 3. Toast the seeds for 8 to 10 minutes, shaking the pan halfway through, until they are lightly warm and fragrant. Do not let them brown too deeply, as this can damage their delicate fats. 4. Transfer the warm pumpkin seeds to the bowl of a high-speed food processor. 5. Process the seeds on high speed for 3 to 4 minutes. They will transform from a dry powder into a thick, clumpy paste. Scrape down the sides of the bowl as needed. 6. Add the avocado oil, Celtic sea salt, and ground vanilla bean to the processor. 7. Continue processing for another 3 to 5 minutes until the friction of the blade releases the seeds' natural oils, yielding an incredibly smooth, glossy, green seed butter. 8. Pour the butter into a clean glass jar and let it cool completely before sealing. Store in the refrigerator for up to 1 month.

Chef's Tip

Using sprouted pumpkin seeds is key. Sprouting neutralizes the naturally occurring enzyme inhibitors and phytic acid, ensuring maximum absorption of anxiety-relieving magnesium.

Tulsi Hibiscus Shrub (Adrenal Cocktail Base)

This tart, bright-red infusion combines holy basil (tulsi)—a famous herb for leveling out wild cortisol spikes—with vitamin C-heavy hibiscus. Your adrenals actually hold more vitamin C than almost any other organ in your body, and they eat it up fast when you're overwhelmed. When you mix these plants with raw apple cider vinegar, you get a powerful, sugar-stabilizing tonic that you can use as the ultimate foundation for your daily mocktails.

Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 10 mins | Servings: 16 (2 tbsp per serving)

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup dried hibiscus flowers
  • 1/4 cup organic dried tulsi (holy basil) leaves
  • 1 cup filtered water
  • 1/2 cup raw honey or maple syrup
  • 1 cup raw, unfiltered apple cider vinegar (with "the mother")

Instructions

1. Combine the dried hibiscus flowers, dried tulsi leaves, and filtered water in a small saucepan over medium heat. 2. Bring the mixture to a gentle simmer, then immediately lower the heat. Cover and let simmer for 10 minutes to create a concentrated herbal tea. 3. Remove the pan from the heat and let it cool until it is lukewarm. 4. Stir in the raw honey or maple syrup until completely dissolved. 5. Pour the raw apple cider vinegar into the pot and stir well to integrate. 6. Pour the entire mixture (including the flowers and leaves) into a clean, wide-mouth quart-sized glass jar. Seal with a plastic lid (or place parchment paper under a metal lid to prevent corrosion from the vinegar). 7. Store the jar in a cool, dark cabinet for 1 week, shaking it daily to infuse. 8. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve, pressing down on the botanicals to extract all the liquid. Discard the solids and bottle the shrub. Store in the refrigerator for up to 3 months.

Chef's Tip

To support your adrenals in the afternoon, mix 2 tablespoons of this shrub with sparkling water, a pinch of Celtic sea salt, and a splash of coconut water for an instant, mineral-rich "adrenal cocktail."

Spinach and Feta Baked Egg Muffins

Think of these as your healthy, homemade answer to those expensive sous-vide egg bites from the drive-thru. They pack a wallop of high-quality protein and choline—which your brain uses to build neurotransmitters—without any of the weird chemical preservatives. Plus, you can make them on Sunday and grab them on your way out the door all week.

Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 20 mins | Servings: 6

Ingredients

  • 10 large pasture-raised eggs
  • 2 cups fresh spinach, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper

Instructions

1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and generously grease a 12-cup muffin tin with olive oil. 2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk the eggs until completely smooth and slightly frothy. 3. Stir in the chopped spinach, crumbled feta, garlic powder, sea salt, and black pepper. 4. Distribute the egg mixture evenly among the 12 muffin cups, filling each about three-quarters full. 5. Bake for 18 to 20 minutes, or until the muffins are set in the center and lightly golden on top. 6. Allow the muffins to cool in the pan for 5 minutes before using a butter knife to gently release them.

Chef's Tip

To prevent soggy egg muffins, squeeze your chopped spinach in a clean paper towel after washing to get rid of any excess water before folding it into the eggs.

Smoked Salmon and Avocado Breakfast Salad

Salad for breakfast? Absolutely. If you’ve ever felt heavy and sluggish after a traditional breakfast of toast or pancakes, this is your antidote. The healthy fats from the avocado and the wild-caught salmon act like a slow-burning log on a fire, giving you steady, clean energy that carries you straight through to lunchtime without a single craving.

Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 0 mins | Servings: 2

Ingredients

  • 6 ounces wild-caught smoked salmon, torn into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 large avocado, pitted and sliced
  • 4 cups baby arugula or mixed baby greens
  • 1/2 English cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons capers, drained
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped

Instructions

1. Arrange the baby arugula or mixed greens across two wide serving bowls as a base. 2. In a small glass jar, whisk the olive oil, fresh lemon juice, and chopped dill together to form a light dressing. 5. Drizzle the dressing over the salads and finish with a sprinkle of flaky sea salt.

Chef's Tip

Look for cold-smoked wild sockeye or coho salmon to get the highest concentration of anti-inflammatory omega-3 fatty acids.

Turmeric Scrambled Tofu with Spinach

You don't need to eat eggs to get a high-protein, adrenal-friendly start to your day. This savory, golden scramble is packed with turmeric. The secret here is the pinch of black pepper—it actually helps your body absorb the active compounds in turmeric so you can fight off the physical wear-and-tear of daily stress. It's warm, comforting, and incredibly quick to throw together.

Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 10 mins | Servings: 2

Ingredients

  • 14 ounces extra-firm tofu, drained and pressed
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 2 cups fresh baby spinach
  • 2 tablespoons nutritional yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt

Instructions

1. Use your hands or a fork to crumble the pressed tofu into bite-sized pieces that look like scrambled eggs. 2. Heat the olive oil in a non-stick skillet over medium heat. 3. Add the crumbled tofu to the skillet and sauté for 3 to 4 minutes until warmed through and excess moisture evaporates. 4. Sprinkle the ground turmeric, black pepper, garlic powder, nutritional yeast, and sea salt over the tofu, stirring well to distribute the spices evenly. 5. Toss in the baby spinach and cook for another 1 to 2 minutes until the leaves are just wilted. 6. Serve warm alongside roasted root vegetables or a slice of gluten-free sourdough bread.

Chef's Tip

Pressing the tofu for at least 15 minutes before cooking removes excess water, allowing the tofu to absorb the warming spices much better.

Nutrient-Dense Beef and Herb Breakfast Patties

If you are dealing with deep, bone-weary fatigue, your body is likely begging for iron and B-vitamins. Grass-fed beef is one of the easiest ways to give your adrenal glands the raw materials they need to make hormones. These savory patties taste like traditional breakfast sausage, but without the mystery fillers, sugar, or low-grade oils.

Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 12 mins | Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 pound grass-fed ground beef
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage, minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, grated
  • 1 teaspoon fine sea salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon ghee or coconut oil (for the pan)

Instructions

1. In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, minced rosemary, minced sage, grated garlic, sea salt, and black pepper. 2. Use your hands to gently mix the ingredients together until just combined, taking care not to overwork the meat. 3. Divide the mixture into 8 equal portions and shape them into small, flat patties about half an inch thick. 4. Melt the ghee or coconut oil in a cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat. 5. Place the patties in the skillet, working in batches if necessary to avoid crowding. 6. Cook for 5 to 6 minutes per side, or until a deeply browned crust forms and the internal temperature reaches 160°F (71°C).

Chef's Tip

Make a double batch of these patties over the weekend and freeze them with parchment paper in between for a quick, high-protein morning reheat.

Asparagus and Goat Cheese Frittata

There's something incredibly comforting about a warm skillet breakfast. This frittata combines crisp asparagus and creamy goat cheese for a meal that feels like a fancy weekend brunch, even on a busy Tuesday. Asparagus is fantastic for your gut microbiome, and since your gut and your brain are constantly talking to each other, a happy gut means a calmer nervous system.

Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 25 mins | Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 8 large pasture-raised eggs
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened coconut milk or whole milk
  • 1 cup asparagus spears, woody ends trimmed, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1/2 cup soft goat cheese, crumbled
  • 1 tablespoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh chives, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper

Instructions

1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). 2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, sea salt, and black pepper until completely combined and airy. 3. Heat the olive oil in a 10-inch oven-safe skillet (preferably cast-iron) over medium heat. 4. Add the asparagus pieces to the skillet and sauté for 3 to 4 minutes until they turn bright green and slightly tender. 5. Pour the egg mixture directly over the asparagus, tilting the pan to ensure the vegetables are evenly distributed. 6. Cook on the stovetop for 2 minutes without stirring, allowing the bottom edge to set. 7. Scatter the crumbled goat cheese and chopped chives over the top of the eggs. 8. Transfer the skillet to the preheated oven and bake for 12 to 15 minutes, or until the center is set and no longer jiggles.

Chef's Tip

Slice the frittata only after it has rested for 5 minutes out of the oven to ensure clean, neat wedges that hold their shape.

Savory Oatmeal with Bone Broth and Poached Egg

If you've only ever had oatmeal with brown sugar and fruit, prepare to have your mind blown. Sweet oatmeal often triggers a massive insulin spike, leaving you starving by 10:00 AM. This savory version cooks the oats in mineral-rich bone broth instead of water or milk, then tops it with a rich, runny poached egg. It's warm, deeply satisfying, and keeps your blood sugar flat.

Prep time: 5 mins | Cook time: 15 mins | Servings: 2

Ingredients

  • 1 cup gluten-free rolled oats
  • 2 cups organic beef or chicken bone broth
  • 2 large pasture-raised eggs
  • 1 tablespoon tamari or coconut aminos
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame oil
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 teaspoon toasted sesame seeds
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar (for poaching eggs)

Instructions

1. In a medium saucepan, bring the bone broth to a gentle boil over medium-high heat. 2. Stir in the rolled oats, reduce the heat to low, and simmer for 8 to 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the oats are creamy and have absorbed most of the liquid. 3. Remove the oats from the heat, stir in the tamari and toasted sesame oil, and divide the mixture into two bowls. 4. To poach the eggs, bring a separate small pot of water to a gentle simmer and stir in the white vinegar. 5. Crack one egg into a small cup, swirl the simmering water to create a gentle whirlpool, and slip the egg into the center. 6. Cook for 3 minutes, then remove with a slotted spoon and repeat with the second egg. 7. Top each bowl of savory oats with a poached egg, sliced green onions, and a sprinkle of toasted sesame seeds.

Chef's Tip

Using homemade or high-quality, gelatin-rich bone broth instead of water introduces collagen and amino acids like glycine, which support gut lining integrity and lower systemic stress.

Pumpkin Seed and Chia Pudding

When your adrenals are exhausted, your digestion often slows down too. This make-ahead pudding does the hard work for you. Soaking the chia and pumpkin seeds overnight breaks down their outer coatings, making them incredibly easy on your stomach. They're loaded with minerals like magnesium, which acts like a natural chill-pill for your nervous system.

Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 0 mins (plus chilling) | Servings: 2

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup chia seeds
  • 1/4 cup raw pumpkin seeds (pepitas)
  • 1 1/2 cups unsweetened almond or light coconut milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup raw walnuts, chopped (for serving)
  • A tiny pinch of fine sea salt

Instructions

1. In a medium glass bowl or quart-sized mason jar, combine the chia seeds, raw pumpkin seeds, ground cinnamon, and sea salt. 2. Pour in the unsweetened nut milk and vanilla extract, stirring vigorously with a whisk or fork. 3. Let the mixture sit at room temperature for 5 minutes, then stir thoroughly once more to prevent any seeds from clumping at the bottom. 4. Cover the container and place it in the refrigerator to set for at least 4 hours, or ideally overnight. 5. To serve, stir the pudding well to distribute the seeds, spoon into bowls, and garnish with the chopped walnuts.

Chef's Tip

If you prefer a smoother, custard-like consistency, blend the entire mixture in a high-speed blender for 30 seconds before refrigerating.

Turkey and Zucchini Breakfast Hash

When you need a warm, savory breakfast but want to keep things light, this quick skillet hash is your best friend. Ground turkey is an excellent source of clean, lean protein, and the diced zucchini adds bulk and hydration without spiking your blood sugar. It's simple, earthy, and cooks in less than twenty minutes.

Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 15 mins | Servings: 3

Ingredients

  • 1 pound lean ground turkey

The Layered French Lentil Mason Jar

1. Toss your walnuts into a dry skillet over medium-low heat. Shake the pan often and toast them for 3 to 4 minutes until you can smell them and they look lightly golden. Let them cool. 2. Drop the cooled walnuts, garlic, and coarse sea salt into your food processor. Pulse a few times until they're finely chopped. 3. Toss in the basil leaves and lemon juice, and pulse until you have a rough, chunky paste. 4. With the processor running, slowly pour the olive oil down the feed tube in a steady stream. You want it mixed, but not baby-food smooth—keep some texture in there. 5. Stir in the grated Pecorino and black pepper by hand, or pulse once just to mix. 6. Scoop it into a jar and pour a thin layer of olive oil over the top to keep the air out and preserve that bright green color.

Chef's Tip

Want your pesto to stay bright green instead of turning a muddy brown? Drop the fresh basil leaves into boiling water for exactly five seconds, then plunge them straight into ice water. Squeeze them dry in a towel before blending. It locks the color in beautifully.

Ginger Turmeric Vinaigrette

This golden dressing gets its punch from fresh ginger and turmeric—two powerhouses used for thousands of years in Ayurvedic medicine to help the body adapt to stress. It has a sharp, warming bite that gets your digestive juices flowing (which usually grind to a halt when you're stressed). It’s perfect for cold winter greens or poured over roasted sweet potatoes and parsnips.

Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 0 mins | Servings: 6

Ingredients

  • 1/3 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 2/3 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tbsp fresh ginger root, finely grated
  • 1 tsp fresh turmeric root, finely grated
  • 1 tbsp raw honey
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1/8 tsp freshly cracked black pepper

Instructions

1. In a small bowl, whisk the apple cider vinegar, grated ginger, grated turmeric, raw honey, Dijon mustard, sea salt, and black pepper until the honey dissolves. 2. Slowly pour in the olive oil while whisking like mad to get the oil and vinegar to bond. 3. Even easier: throw everything into a small glass jar, screw the lid on tight, and shake it like a paint mixer for 30 seconds. 4. Let the dressing sit on the counter for 10 minutes before using. This gives the fresh ginger and turmeric time to steep into the oil. 5. Keep it in the fridge for up to five days. It will separate as it sits, so just give it a good shake before you pour.

Chef's Tip

Never skip the black pepper when you're using turmeric. The piperine in black pepper helps your body absorb the active curcumin in turmeric by up to 2,000 percent. They’re a package deal.

Fermented Red Onions

Keeping a jar of these bright pink onions in your fridge is an easy win. Fermenting onions changes them from sharp and heavy to sweet, tangy, and crunchy. More importantly, the natural fermentation process creates gut-friendly probiotics. Since your gut and brain are constantly talking to each other, a happy microbiome directly translates to a calmer mind. Pile these onto salads, wraps, or roasted meat.

Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 0 mins (plus 24 hours fermenting) | Servings: 12

Ingredients

  • 2 large red onions, very thinly sliced
  • 1 cup filtered water
  • 1/2 cup raw apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp fine sea salt
  • 1 tbsp raw honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled and smashed
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns

Instructions

1. Pack your sliced red onions, smashed garlic, and peppercorns tightly into a clean pint-sized mason jar. 2. Whisk the water, apple cider vinegar, salt, and honey in a measuring cup until the salt and honey disappear. 3. Pour this liquid over the onions. Make sure they are completely covered. 4. Use a clean spoon to press the onions down into the jar to nudge out any trapped air bubbles. 5. Set the lid on the jar loosely (don't crank it tight, as gases need to escape) and let it sit on your kitchen counter out of direct sunlight for 24 hours. 6. Tighten the lid and move it to the fridge. The onions will get tastier as they sit and will keep for up to three weeks.

Chef's Tip

Use a mandoline to slice the onions paper-thin. It helps them soften up quickly and absorb all that mineral-rich brine evenly.

Sunflower Seed Butter Spread

This is a rich, nut-free spread that is loaded with magnesium and vitamin E—antioxidants that help protect your cells from stress-induced wear and tear. It has a deep, toasted flavor that makes a highly satisfying alternative to supermarket peanut butter, which is often full of hydrogenated oils and sugar. Slather it on grain-free toast or use it as a dip for crisp green apple slices when the 3 PM cravings hit.

Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 10 mins | Servings: 10

Ingredients

  • 3 cups raw sunflower seeds
  • 2 tbsp avocado oil
  • 1 tbsp raw honey
  • 1/2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon

Instructions

1. Heat your oven to 325°F (160°C). Spread the sunflower seeds out on a baking sheet. 2. Roast for 8 to 10 minutes, giving them a shake halfway through, until they are fragrant and golden. Let them cool until they're warm, not hot. 3. Dump the warm seeds into a food processor and run it on high for 4 to 5 minutes. Stop occasionally to scrape down the sides. 4. Be patient here. The seeds will go from a dry powder, to a gritty paste, and then—suddenly—they'll release their oils and turn into a glossy, liquid butter. 5. Once it's smooth, add the avocado oil, raw honey, salt, and cinnamon. 6. Run the processor for another minute or two until everything is velvety and well mixed. 7. Pour it into a clean glass jar and store it in the fridge for up to three weeks.

Chef's Tip

Don't rush the blending process. It takes a few minutes for the seeds to warm up and release their natural fats. If you panic and add liquid too early, the butter will seize up into a dry, clumpy block.

Lemon Herb Dressing

This is the ultimate everyday Mediterranean dressing. Fresh lemon juice gives your adrenal glands a quick hit of vitamin C (which they consume rapidly when you're stressed), while the fresh herbs bring a payload of protective antioxidants. It's clean, bright, and takes under five minutes to whip up.

Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 0 mins | Servings: 6

Ingredients

  • 1/3 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 2/3 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 cup fresh flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp fresh oregano, finely chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, finely grated
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tsp fine sea salt
  • 1/4 tsp freshly cracked black pepper

Instructions

1. Whisk the lemon juice, chopped parsley, chopped oregano, grated garlic, mustard, sea salt, and black pepper in a small bowl. 2. Drizzle in the olive oil slowly while whisking constantly to build an emulsion. 3. Or, throw everything into a jar, seal it, and shake it hard until it looks creamy and uniform. 4. Taste it. Adjust the salt or lemon juice to get it exactly how you like it. 5. Use it right away, or store it in the fridge for up to 5 days. Just give it a shake before using.*

Chef's Tip

Don't chop the garlic; grate it on a microplane. It blends seamlessly into the oil so you get the flavor in every bite without biting into raw, pungent chunks.
