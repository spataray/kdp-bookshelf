Ginger-Mint Electrolyte Hydration

When your stomach feels like a washing machine on the spin cycle, this is your emergency brake. The ginger cuts through nausea with clinical precision, while the mint cools down the fiery, acidic sensation that often creeps up when your stomach is empty.

Prep time: 5 mins | Cook time: 0 mins | Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 4 cups filtered water
  • 1 cup pure coconut water
  • 1 inch fresh ginger, thinly sliced
  • 10 fresh mint leaves, gently bruised
  • 2 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 1/4 tsp Celtic sea salt

Instructions

1. Muddle the ginger slices and bruised mint leaves in the bottom of a large glass pitcher. Don't pulverize them; just crush them enough to release those crucial volatile oils. 2. Pour in the filtered water, coconut water, and fresh lime juice, stirring vigorously. 3. Whisk in the Celtic sea salt until fully dissolved. 4. Let the mixture steep in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour before straining. Sip in 4-ounce portions throughout the day.

Chef's Tip

Gently bruise the mint leaves by clapping them firmly between your flat hands; this releases the aromatic oils without shredding the herb, keeping your drink clean and free of bitter debris.

Low-FODMAP Chicken Bone Broth

On the days when the mere thought of solid food makes you want to curl into a ball, this broth is your lifesaver. We’ve stripped out the garlic and onions because when your digestion operates at a crawl, brassicas and alliums ferment in your gut, causing painful bloating. Instead, we use fresh ginger to soothe the stomach and pasture-raised bones to deliver pure, liquid collagen.

Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 12 hours | Servings: 6

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs grass-fed, pasture-raised chicken carcasses or bones
  • 2 medium carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 1 inch fresh ginger, sliced
  • 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1 bunch fresh parsley
  • 12 cups filtered water
  • 1 tbsp sea salt

Instructions

1. Place the chicken bones, carrots, celery, and ginger into a large stockpot or slow cooker. 2. Pour the filtered water and apple cider vinegar over the ingredients, ensuring the bones are completely submerged. Let sit for 20 minutes to draw out minerals from the bone matrix. 3. Bring the mixture to a low boil over medium-high heat, then immediately reduce the heat to the lowest setting. Cover and simmer gently for 12 hours (or 4 hours in a pressure cooker on high pressure). 4. During the last 30 minutes of cooking, stir in the fresh parsley. 5. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve, discard the solids, stir in the sea salt, and allow to cool completely before storing in glass jars.

Chef's Tip

To maximize the gelatin extraction, look for chicken bones with plenty of connective tissue, such as chicken backs, necks, or feet. If the cooled broth jiggles like Jell-O, you've done it perfectly.

Mineral-Rich Beef Bone Broth

Rapid weight loss is a double-edged sword. If you aren't careful, your body will happily burn through lean muscle mass instead of fat. This rich, deeply roasted beef broth is a powerhouse of amino acids that tells your body, "we are not starving, we are thriving." The marrow bones provide a dense profile of essential minerals that support your joints and keep your energy levels steady.

Prep time: 20 mins | Cook time: 24 hours | Servings: 8

Ingredients

  • 3 lbs grass-fed beef marrow and knuckle bones
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 2 medium carrots, cut into chunks
  • 2 stalks celery, cut into chunks
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 14 cups filtered water
  • 1 tbsp sea salt

Instructions

1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Arrange the beef bones on a baking sheet and roast for 30 minutes until deeply browned and fragrant. 2. Transfer the roasted bones to a large stockpot or slow cooker, scraping any browned, caramelized bits from the baking sheet into the pot. 3. Add the carrots, celery, peppercorns, bay leaves, apple cider vinegar, and water. 4. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to a low, steady simmer. Cover and cook for 24 hours, occasionally skimming any foam from the surface. 5. Strain the broth through a cheesecloth-lined sieve, stir in the sea salt, and let cool. Refrigerate overnight, then scrape off the hardened fat layer from the top before reheating.

Chef's Tip

Do not skip roasting the beef bones; this step caramelizes the proteins and sugars, which is absolutely essential for developing a rich, rounded umami flavor.

Umami Shiitake Kelp Broth

This is a plant-based mineral bomb inspired by Japanese dashi. If you’re tired of heavy animal broths, this clean, earthy alternative provides an incredible dose of natural iodine from kombu, which supports your thyroid function during metabolic changes, alongside the deep, savory satisfaction of shiitake mushrooms.

Prep time: 5 mins | Cook time: 30 mins | Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 1 piece Kombu (dried kelp, roughly 4x4 inches)
  • 6 dried shiitake mushrooms
  • 1 inch fresh ginger, sliced
  • 1 tbsp low-sodium tamari or coconut aminos
  • 6 cups filtered water

Instructions

1. Place the Kombu, dried shiitake mushrooms, ginger, and filtered water into a medium saucepan. 2. Heat the liquid over medium-low heat until it reaches a bare simmer. Do not let it boil, as boiling the Kombu can make the broth bitter and slimy. 3. Remove the Kombu slice immediately once the water begins to simmer, then reduce heat to low and simmer the mushrooms for 25 minutes. 4. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve, keeping the mushrooms for other culinary uses if desired. 5. Stir in the tamari or coconut aminos and serve warm.

Chef's Tip

Wipe the Kombu gently with a damp cloth before cooking, but do not wash off the white powder on its surface; those are natural glutamate crystals that deliver the deep umami flavor.

Soothing Ginger-Pear Egg White Omelet

This delicate, mildly sweet omelet pairs the warmth of fresh ginger with the soft texture of ripe pear. It is incredibly easy on the stomach while delivering clean, lean protein that digests without effort.

Prep time: 5 mins | Cook time: 8 mins | Servings: 1

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup liquid egg whites
  • 1/4 cup finely diced ripe Bosc pear
  • 1/2 tsp freshly grated ginger
  • 1 tsp coconut oil
  • 1 pinch sea salt

Instructions

1. Heat the coconut oil in a small, non-stick skillet over medium-low heat. 2. Toss in the diced pear and grated ginger. Sauté for 2 to 3 minutes. You want the pear pieces soft, warm, and fragrant. 3. Pour the egg whites evenly over the top, swirling the pan to coat the bottom. 4. Sprinkle with a pinch of sea salt. Let it cook undisturbed for about 3 minutes until the edges turn opaque and lift easily. 5. Fold the omelet in half, let the center set for one more minute, and slide it gently onto your plate.

Chef's Tip

Don't grab a soft Bartlett pear for this. Bosc or D'Anjou pears hold their shape when heated, giving you a lovely texture instead of turning into baby food in the pan.

Velvety Soft Scrambled Eggs with Fresh Chives

This is the ultimate comforting breakfast. By cooking the eggs low and slow, we avoid the rubbery, sulfurous texture that triggers morning aversion. Instead, you get a custard-like plate that practically melts.

Prep time: 5 mins | Cook time: 6 mins | Servings: 1

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tbsp unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tsp unsalted butter
  • 1 tbsp finely chopped fresh chives
  • 1 pinch sea salt

Instructions

1. Crack the eggs into a small bowl, add the almond milk and sea salt, and whisk with a fork until the mixture is completely uniform and slightly frothy. 2. Melt the butter in a small non-stick skillet over your lowest stove setting. 3. Pour in the eggs and let them sit for exactly 30 seconds. 4. Using a soft silicone spatula, gently sweep the eggs from the outer edges into the center, forming delicate, pillowy folds. 5. Keep stirring slowly over low heat for 3 to 4 minutes. The moment they look creamy and mostly set, remove them from the burner. 6. Fold in the fresh chives and slide them onto a warm plate immediately.

Chef's Tip

The secret to perfect soft scrambles is pulling them off the heat while they still look a little wet. The residual heat in the eggs will finish cooking them on the plate so they don't dry out.

Silky Japanese-Style Steamed Egg Custard

Inspired by traditional chawanmushi, this savory custard is incredibly gentle on a delicate digestive tract. The combination of steamed egg and lean chicken slides down effortlessly while delivering clean, restorative protein.

Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 12 mins | Servings: 1

Ingredients

  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup warm chicken broth
  • 2 tbsp cooked minced chicken breast
  • 1 tsp tamari or soy sauce
  • 1 small sprig fresh cilantro (optional)

Instructions

1. In a small bowl, whisk the egg, warm broth, and tamari together gently. Try not to whip air into it; you want a smooth liquid, not a frothy one. 2. Place the minced chicken at the bottom of a small, heatproof ceramic ramekin. 3. Pour the egg mixture through a fine-mesh strainer directly over the chicken. 4. Cover the ramekin tightly with a piece of aluminum foil. 5. Place the ramekin into a pot filled with 1 inch of boiling water. Cover the pot with its lid and steam over medium-low heat for 12 minutes. The custard should be set but have a slight jiggle in the center.

Chef's Tip

Passing the egg mixture through a fine strainer removes the chalazae (the stringy white part of the egg), which is the key to achieving that flawless, melt-in-your-mouth texture.

Bake-And-Go Spinach and Feta Egg Bites

These velvety, oven-baked egg bites are your insurance policy for busy mornings. Packed with iron and tangy feta, they offer a savory, grab-and-go protein boost that reheats beautifully.

Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 20 mins | Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup low-fat cottage cheese
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh baby spinach
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper

Instructions

1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and place a silicone egg bite mold onto a sturdy baking sheet. 2. Put the eggs, cottage cheese, and black pepper into a blender. Blending the eggs with cottage cheese is the ultimate culinary hack for copying those famous coffee-shop egg bites. It gives them an incredibly rich, velvety texture without adding heavy cream.

Lemon-Herb Steamed Cod

There is a reason coastal Mediterranean cooks rely so heavily on white fish. It’s light, clean, and requires almost zero digestive effort. Steaming keeps the cod incredibly moist, making it a perfect choice for those days when your stomach is feeling particularly sensitive.

Prep time: 10 mins | Cook time: 12 mins | Servings: 2

Ingredients

  • 8 oz wild-caught cod fillet (cut into two 4-oz portions)
  • 1/2 lemon, thinly sliced
  • 2 sprigs fresh dill
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 1 tsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 tsp sea salt
  • 1/8 tsp freshly cracked black pepper

Instructions

1. Set up a steamer basket in a saucepan with an inch of simmering water. 2. Season the cod fillets with sea salt and black pepper, then drizzle with olive oil. 3. Place the cod in the steamer basket, topping each portion with fresh dill, thyme, and lemon slices. 4. Cover tightly and steam for 10–12 minutes until the fish is opaque and flakes easily with a fork.

Chef's Tip

Don't throw away the juices at the bottom of the steamer basket. Line the steamer with a bed of baby spinach; it will wilt beautifully in the fish's natural juices, creating an instant, nutrient-packed side dish.

This streamlined, home-friendly version centers around a deeply aromatic beef broth infused with warm spices like star anise and cinnamon—spices that are famous for calming an irritated stomach. By pouring boiling hot broth directly over raw, paper-thin beef slices, you cook the meat instantly, keeping it tender and easy to digest rather than tough and chewy.

Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 45 mins | Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 6 cups Beef bone broth
  • 1 Star anise pod
  • 1 small Cinnamon stick
  • 1 inch Fresh ginger, sliced and bruised
  • 4 oz Sirloin steak, frozen for 30 minutes and sliced paper-thin
  • 2 oz Thin rice noodles (pho noodles)
  • 1 tbsp Fish sauce
  • 1/2 cup Fresh cilantro leaves
  • 1/2 cup Fresh Thai basil or sweet basil leaves

Instructions

1. Toast the star anise and cinnamon stick in a dry soup pot over medium heat for 2 minutes until highly fragrant. 2. Add the beef bone broth, ginger slices, and fish sauce. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer uncovered for 30 minutes to infuse the spices. 3. While the broth simmers, cook the rice noodles according to package instructions, drain, and divide them among four serving bowls. 4. Strain the hot broth to remove the spices and ginger, then return the clear broth to the pot and bring it to a rolling boil. 5. Arrange the raw, paper-thin sirloin slices over the noodles in each bowl, then immediately ladle the boiling hot broth over the beef (the heat of the broth will cook the beef instantly). 6. Garnish with cilantro and basil before serving.

Chef's Tip

Trying to slice raw beef paper-thin is an exercise in frustration. Do this instead: throw the steak in the freezer for about 30 minutes before you plan to cook. It firms up the muscle fibers just enough so your knife can glide right through, yielding perfect, restaurant-style ribbons.

Ingredients

Greek Yogurt Panna Cotta

Forget the rubbery, artificial desserts of the past. This is silky, elegant, and secretly packed with high-quality protein. By swapping out heavy cream for strained Greek yogurt, you get a gut-friendly dessert that feels like a cheat meal but actually preserves your muscles.

Prep time: 15 mins | Cook time: 5 mins | Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 1.5 cups Plain Greek yogurt (use 2% or 5% fat, brought to room temperature)
  • 1/2 cup Unsweetened vanilla almond milk
  • 1.5 tsp Unflavored gelatin powder
  • 3 tbsp Pure maple syrup
  • 1 tsp Vanilla bean paste (or pure vanilla extract)

Instructions

1. Pour the almond milk into a small saucepan and sprinkle the gelatin powder evenly over the top. Let it sit untouched for 5 minutes. You'll see the gelatin soften and wrinkly—this is called "blooming." 2. Put the saucepan over low heat. Stir constantly for about 2 to 3 minutes until the gelatin is completely dissolved. In a medium bowl, whisk your room-temperature Greek yogurt until it's perfectly smooth. Slowly stream the warm gelatin mixture into the yogurt, whisking continuously to make sure no lumps form. 5. Pour the mixture into four small ramekins or dessert glasses. 6. Cover them and chill in the fridge for at least 4 hours, or until they are set and cold.

Chef's Tip

If you use ice-cold yogurt straight from the fridge, the warm gelatin will seize up the moment it hits the bowl, leaving you with a gritty, lumpy texture. Let the yogurt sit on the counter for 30 minutes first.

Ingredients

Gelatin-Rich Umami Gravy

Most gravies are thickened with heavy flour and fat, which can feel like lead in a slow-moving stomach. It is a savory, deeply comforting sauce that is incredibly easy to digest and packed with amino acids that support your gut lining.

Prep time: 5 mins | Cook time: 15 mins | Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 2 cups high-quality beef or chicken bone broth
  • 1 tablespoon grass-fed butter or ghee
  • 1 small shallot, finely minced
  • 1 tablespoon arrowroot powder
  • 1 tablespoon cold water
  • 1 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon coconut aminos or tamari
  • Sea salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions

1. Melt the butter or ghee in a small saucepan over medium heat. Sauté the minced shallot for 3 to 4 minutes until it is soft, translucent, and smells sweet. 2. Chef's Tip

Arrowroot powder is a superior thickener for sensitive stomachs because it is completely grain-free. However, it loses its thickening power if boiled aggressively, so make sure to keep the heat low once you stir in the slurry.
